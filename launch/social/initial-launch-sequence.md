# My Kingdom Pal Launch Social Sequence

## Campaign Summary
This package turns the launch plan into a concrete 3-week pre-launch sequence for church customers.

Primary audience on both Instagram and LinkedIn:
- Pastors
- Ministry leaders
- Church staff
- Church planters
- Ministry-minded operators and strategic partners

Primary goal:
- Build awareness and credibility with churches before launch

Core positioning:
`My Kingdom Pal is a church-led spiritual formation app that helps churches carry Sunday into the rest of the week through gentle daily discipleship, private reflection, and care connection.`

Core message interfaces:
- Brand line: `Walk with the Word all week.`
- Support line: `Helping believers live the Word every day, beyond Sunday.`
- Product category: `Church-led spiritual formation app`
- Customer framing: `Built for churches to extend Sunday into the week`
- Privacy line: `Private by default`
- Care line: `Supports prayer and care connection when members choose to share`

## LinkedIn Post 1
### Goal
State the product thesis clearly for church leaders and partners.

### Format
Text-led post with one strong image

### Post Copy
Many churches are strong on Sunday and still under-supported Monday through Saturday.

That isn't a criticism. It's a real ministry constraint.

A sermon can be faithful, clear, and powerful. But carrying that message into the rest of the week usually depends on disconnected habits, scattered notes, or no shared rhythm at all.

My Kingdom Pal is a church-led spiritual formation app built to help churches carry Sunday into the rest of the week through gentle daily discipleship, private reflection, and care connection.

We're building it with a few clear convictions:
- spiritual formation should not feel like a streak app
- private reflection should stay private by default
- technology should support the church, not compete with it

We're getting closer to launch, and the goal is simple: help churches create a quieter, more consistent rhythm of weekday discipleship.

If you lead in ministry, I'd be interested to hear how your church thinks about formation between Sundays.

## LinkedIn Post 3
### Goal
Establish conviction and founder-level credibility.

### Format
Founder-note style post with static brand visual

### Post Copy
Many digital products are designed to maximize frequency, retention, and visible engagement.

That logic makes sense in a lot of categories. I don't think it translates cleanly to spiritual formation.

If the goal is to help churches support people beyond Sunday, then the product should probably feel quieter, more trustworthy, and less performative.

That's part of what is shaping My Kingdom Pal.

We're building around a few convictions:
- the local church should remain the center of spiritual leadership
- weekly formation should flow from Sunday teaching, not compete with it
- private reflection should stay private by default
- prayer and care moments should have a clearer path to follow-up when people choose to share

The problem we're trying to solve is not just content access. It's continuity.

How does a church help people carry the Word into the rest of the week in a way that feels pastoral, grounded, and sustainable?

That's the question behind the product.

If you're working on this kind of problem in your church, I'd be glad to connect before launch.

## Production Notes
- Keep all church-facing language explicit in the first slide or first two lines
- Use end-user screenshots only as proof of church value
- Avoid claims about analytics, dashboards, automations, or church admin tooling not currently present in the product
- Keep all launch timing language flexible: `launching soon`, `as we get closer`, `before launch`
