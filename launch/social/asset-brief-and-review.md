## Message Pillars
### Pillar 1: The Church Problem
- Sunday teaching is powerful, but weekday formation is often under-supported
- Churches need a rhythm that helps members move from sermon to practice
- Reflection and pastoral care are often disconnected from each other

### Pillar 2: The Church-Centered Solution
- Sermon-linked weekly rhythm
- Gentle weekday prompts
- Private-by-default reflection
- Prayer and support pathways when members choose to share

### Pillar 3: Product Convictions
- Church-led, not platform-led
- Non-gamified by design
- Reverent and quiet in tone
- Built to support discipleship and care

## Approved Claims
These claims are supported by the product as currently represented in the repo.

- `My Kingdom Pal is a church-led spiritual formation app`
- `It helps churches carry Sunday into the rest of the week`
- `It is built around a gentle weekday rhythm`
- `Reflection is private by default`
- `Journal entries and mood notes are designed to remain on the device`
- `Prayer and support requests can create a path for church follow-up`
- `The product is intentionally non-gamified`
- `The experience is designed to support the local church, not replace it`

## Use-With-Care Claims
These can be used only with careful wording.

- `Supports pastoral care`
  - Use only when paired with `when members choose to share`
- `Helps churches disciple people through the week`
  - Use as vision and positioning, not as a guaranteed outcome
- `Built for churches launching soon`
  - Safe if there is no hard date

## Forbidden Claims
Do not imply any of the following unless the product changes first.

- Church dashboards or admin analytics
- Staff workflow automation
- Full church communication suite
- Real-time pastor monitoring of private reflections
- AI-driven church insights for leaders
- Multi-church management
- Measurable discipleship outcomes
- Launch-date certainty if the date is not locked
- Public app store availability unless it is live

## Review Checklist
### Messaging Review
- A church leader can tell within one slide or two lines that the post is for churches
- The post names a real ministry problem
- The post positions end-user experience as evidence of church value, not the main audience frame
- The post does not drift into consumer-app language

### Product Accuracy Review
- Every feature claim is visible in the current app or clearly framed as launch-near
- Privacy language matches the current local-first implementation
- Care language is conditional and truthful
- No admin or analytics features are implied

### Tone Review
- Warm and grounded, not hype-driven
- Ministry-aware without sounding vague or performative
- Clear and restrained
- Appropriate for pastors and ministry leaders on both Instagram and LinkedIn

### Design Review
- First slide headline is readable on mobile
- Copy stays sparse and legible
- Screenshot framing makes the church value obvious
- Midnight-and-gold palette stays consistent with the app
- No slide looks like generic SaaS content

### Launch Readiness Review
- CTA matches a real next step
- Any links mentioned are live
- Timing language remains flexible if launch shifts
