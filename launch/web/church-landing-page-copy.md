# My Kingdom Pal Church Landing Page Copy

## Purpose
This page is the updated website copy foundation for a church-facing launch or waitlist page.

It is written to replace older product framing with messaging aligned to the current app and launch sequence.

Primary audience:
- Pastors
- Executive pastors
- Ministry leaders
- Church staff
- Church planters

Primary goal:
- Help church decision-makers understand what MKP is, why it matters, and why they should follow the launch or request early access

## Messaging Principles
- Speak to church needs first
- Use member experience as proof of church value
- Keep privacy claims precise
- Do not imply admin tooling unless it is actually available
- Keep launch timing flexible unless a date is locked

## FAQ
### Question 1
#### Prompt
`Is My Kingdom Pal built for individual consumers or for churches?`

#### Answer
`The product is being built for churches. Individual member experience matters, but it is framed in service of church-led discipleship and care.`

### Question 2
#### Prompt
`Does the app replace pastoral leadership or church discipleship strategy?`

#### Answer
`No. The goal is to support the church's existing teaching and care rhythm, not replace leadership, pastoral relationships, or ministry structure.`

### Question 3
#### Prompt
`Are member reflections visible to pastors or church staff?`

#### Answer
`Private reflections and mood notes are designed to remain on the member's device. Care-related submissions are only surfaced when members choose to share them for church follow-up.`

### Question 4
#### Prompt
`Is this a church management platform?`

#### Answer
`No. My Kingdom Pal is not being positioned as a full church management or communication suite. It is focused on formation rhythm, private reflection, and care connection.`

### Question 5
#### Prompt
`Can our church request early access?`

#### Answer
`Yes. The launch page should include an early-access or interest form for church leaders once that workflow is ready.`
